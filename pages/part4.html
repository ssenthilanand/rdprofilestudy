IV. Some preliminary results for Anna University

Some preliminary results of our work done so far are given here where the 41 areas are arranged into three layers according to the number of faculty involved in each of them, as below:

Top layer: Areas having Faculty strength above 50 each (the maximum is seen to be 70)
Middle layer: Faculty strength in the range 26 to 50
Bottom layer: Faculty strength in the range 1 to 25 (the lowest is 4)

Each of the 3 broad domains (A,B and C) is shown in one colour. The size of the circle is proportional to the logarithm of the faculty strength, making the circle having the largest number the biggest, and the circle having the least number of faculty as the smallest. On clicking the circles, details of their sub areas (topics) and faculty list are displayed, with the faculty names being linked to their IRINS database entries.

There is also a provision made for searching for a required topic of specialisation by clicking here which returns the list of faculty members involved in that area, linked to their IRINS web pages.

It is emphasised here again that these results are shown only as a PoC, and considerably more work needs to be done to make it more effective and useful. For instance, areas like Mechanical, Computers etc are really not specialisations (as in the sense of Mining or Grid/Cloud Computing), and these will have to be examined as to how and where the items under them can be re-assigned to existing Areas of specialisations, or to new Areas of specialisations.

Also, ordering the Areas (Clusters) in terms of their faculty strengths as shown in the figure is not particularly useful since more faculty does not necessarily mean higher R&D strengths. R&D strengths of different Areas (Clusters) have to be computed using the methods suggested in Section II above, and then ranked accordingly. Unfortunately, IRINS database is not of much use in this regard as mentioned earlier. This will have to be done afresh using techniques of web crawling, information extraction etc from the primary sources of information available elsewhere for the common metrices like Publications, Research Grants, Patents, Theses, etc.
