Analysis and Quantification of the R&D strengths of different Areas and different Faculty Members in a University

An investigation taken up by the AU-KBC Centre and its preliminary Results

Abstract

It is often very difficult to get accurate and authentic information regarding the research strengths of the different faculty members as well as of the different specialised R&D areas in a university, especially large public universities. A computational model and a software tool are proposed here that can be interrogated to yield the required information for universities or other similar institutions. The core of the model is a way of computing the relative R&D score s(i,j) for a faculty member i in a given area j taking into account his/her different contributions like publications, theses, patents, funded activities etc. in that area. The results can be organised to rank faculty members, departments/centres, areas of specialisation etc in the university as per their relative R&D strengths. Clusters of related and connected R&D areas can be identified, along with the faculty members who are active in them, and these clusters can be made the units for planning future directions of research, in place of, or in addition to, just the departments and centres of the university. Some preliminary results of this ongoing investigation using the publicly available IRINS Database (https://irins.org/irins/) are presented.
