Research Profile of Anna University as extracted from the IRINS data base

Some preliminary results of an analysis carried out of the R&D profile of University Departments of Anna University is presented here that gives an overview of the research being done by the faculty in the various areas of engineering, sciences, mathematics and humanities. This is still a work in progress and would be continuously enhanced and updated.

IRINS (https://irins.org/irins/) is a web-based Research Information Management (RIM) service developed recently by the Information and Library Network (INFLIBNET) in collaboration with the Central University of Punjab. Anna University has populated the template supplied by IRINS with data of about 500 of its regular faculty, and the same is available at https://annauniv.irins.org/

One of the information provided by the faculty members in the database is about the topics in which they are specialising, and in all we have identified 956 such distinct topics of specialisation for Anna University, belonging to 3 broad domains:

  1. Engineering: Electrical and Computing Sciences (E&C)
  2. Engineering: Non- E&C areas
  3. Mathematics, Sciences & Humanities, and Management

Through a first cut manual process, we have grouped the 956 topics into 41 clusters or areas of specialisation â€“ 10 belonging to E&C Sciences domain, 28 belonging to non-E&C domain and 3 belonging to Maths, Sciences & Humanities and Management Domains. While the Domains (3) are too broad and Topics (956) are too narrow, the Areas (41) are believed to be of optimum granularity and size for meaningful understanding and interpretation of the R&D activities being carried out by the faculty of Anna University.

Some preliminary results of the analysis are given here in two forms:

1. The Clusters. The 41 clusters (areas of specialisation), along with the constituents of each of them are available here where clusters are arranged into 3 layers according to the number of faculty involved in each of them. Also each of the 3 broad domains (A,B and C) is shown in one colour:

A. Electrical & Computing (E&C) Sciences
B. Non-E&C areas
C. Maths, Sciences, Management & Humanities

The size of the circle is proportional to the logarithm of the faculty strength. On clicking the circles, details of their sub areas (topics) and associated faculty list are displayed, with the faculty names being linked to their IRINS database entries.

Examination of the cluster details (such as AI, Composites, Automation etc.) clearly shows how the existing departments/centres division do not any more represent research specialisations, with each area of specialisation having faculty from 8-10 different departments/centres, spread across different university campuses. Planning and Organisation of R&D in the university may have to be conceived around these clusters instead.

2. The Search Facility Search can be carried out here , either by names of the topics or by faculty names. In place of searching, the 956 topics have also been listed here alphabetically which can be scrolled down to the desired one and see the faculty involved.

Both the clustering and the search facilities are presently being further automated and enhanced, and the improved results will be posted here from time to time.
